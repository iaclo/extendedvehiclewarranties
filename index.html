What Is An Extended Auto Warranty?

An extended car warranty policy covers the cost to repair or replace a car that is under warranty.

The policy typically lasts for a certain number of years and covers many potential problems.

An extended car warranty is an agreement between a car owner and a warranty provider that covers some of the costs involved in repairing or replacing car components after the standard manufacturer's warranty expires.

An extended car warranty covers most items, including appliances, cars, and used items.

Extended car warranties are intended to complement the factory warranty.

This means that it kicks into effect after the standard manufacturer warranty has expired.

Extended warranties also usually cover expenses that are not covered by the factory warranty such as batteries and brake pads.

Keep in mind that while most items are covered, there are some exceptions. A policy that covers extended car warranties does not typically cover wear and tears items like brakes or other regular use.

Mechanical and electrical components are usually covered, but other costs associated repair or replacement (such labor) are not always included.

Third-party companies usually offer extended warranties for an additional cost, but sometimes they're included with the cost of the car and serviced by the manufacturer's dealer.

Be sure to read the fine print before you make a purchase.

It is also worth calculating whether the premium plus the deductible for each repair are worthwhile.
